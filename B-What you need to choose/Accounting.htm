Accounting

In a nutshell:

You will need to keep accounting for your business. Its form and complexity depend on the taxation form chosen and on the level of your revenues.

Forms of accounting

As an entrepreneur, you are responsible for keeping accounting documentation – invoices, bills, receipts, records … They should be duly issued and recorded, as they are a basis for calculating your taxes and other dues.

The available forms of accounting are:

Hire an accountant

You can entrust your accounting to a specialised firm, an accounting office.

  • choose an accounting firm, for example one recommended by your friend, or use an Internet browser.
  • sign an agreement with the accounting firm and other required documents, for example a power of attorney for them.

Then, your duties will be:

  • to provide your accountant with all the accounting documents that you generate or obtain
  • to inform your accountant of any changes in your business that could impact your accounting
  • to pay taxes and other public dues according to the information that you receive from your accountant.

Remember:  

You are solely responsible for timely payment of taxes and other dues.

Be your own accountant

You can keep your accounting, using modern and user-friendly tools. Just search the Internet and choose one of many on-line accounting service providers. Usually they offer a free trial month or a demo version,  so you can test the chosen platform and see if it suits your needs.

The most important features of on-line accounting are:

  • you can choose from one of several packages – depending on the type and scale of your business, whether you employ employees, etc.

  • you pay a monthly or a yearly fee. Usually, its amount depends on the number of documents that you generate in a month

  • you issue your own invoices and scan your cost invoices – they are automatically booked to relevant accounts. The process is simple and does not require any accounting background.

  • you generate your monthly tax returns and social security declarations and send them to the relevant offices via your accounting platform

  • when necessary, you can ask your accountant for advice (for an extra fee).

    What to do:  

    1. Choose an appropriate form of accounting depending on the scale of your business.
    2. Decide whether you will hire a professional accountant or keep your own accounting.
    3. Enter the relevant information in section 17 of your registration application.
