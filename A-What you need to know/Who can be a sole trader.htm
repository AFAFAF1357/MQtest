Who can be a sole trader

In a nutshell:  

Essentially, every individual can start a business

Freedom of business activity

Under the Polish law (The Act of 6 March 2018 on entrepreneurship) everybody has a right to start, run and close a business on equal terms.

Business activity is understood as an organised commercial activity for profit, performed on the entrepreneur’s own behalf and permanently.

In Poland, the following persons can be an entrepreneur:

  • natural person
  • a legal person
  • an organisational unit not being a legal person, having legal capacity under a separate statute, running a business.

Special cases

A foreigner

In a nutshell:  

A foreigner can run a business in Poland. Depending on their country of origin, though, certain conditions need to be met.

You do not need to be a Polish citizen to start a business as a sole trader. The exact requirements depend on your country of origin. See the information in the table below.

If you are a citizen of:

  
A EU or EEA*) member state You can start your business activity in the same way as a Polish citizen
US or Switzerland You can start your business activity in the same way as a Polish citizen
Any other country You need a residency title to start business activity. See below for details

*) The European Economic Area members are: the EU member states and Norway, Iceland, and Liechtenstein

Citizens of other countries

Citizens of countries not being members of the EU or of the European Economic Area can start and run a business in Poland on the same terms as Polish citizens, if they have a residency title giving such right. The relevant titles are listed in Article 4 sec. 2, 4 i 5 of the Act of 6 March 2018 on the principles of participation of foreign entrepreneurs and other foreign persons in business transactions in the Republic of Poland.

Consult the list of the relevant residency titles here.

 

What to do:  

  1. Check the conditions that apply to you.
  2. Prepare the required document(s).
  3. When registering your business, you will need to give the details of the relevant document in section 2 of your registration application.

 

A minor

In a nutshell:

If you are a minor A person under 18 years of age, you will need one of your parents to represent you.

You can start a business in Poland even if you are a minor A person under 18 years of age (you are not 18 years old yet).]

If this is the case, though, you will need to obtain consent or your legal representative (most often your parent or guardian).

As a rule, any parent can represent a child individually. This rule does not apply, though, when one of the parents objected to such representation.

Only your parent can register your business on your behalf with CEIDG Centralna Informacja i Ewidencja Działalności Gospodarczej – the Central Register and Information on Economic Activity – is the Polish office run by the minister in charge of economic matters, where sole traderships and civil-law partnerships are registered. It must be done in person – your parent will need to file a paper application with the local community office.

CEIDG will verify such an application – this means that the procedure may take longer. Besides, if there are any doubts as to the person of the representative, the office can refuse the registration.

In a special case, where you have the consent of a court to open a business, additional verification by CEIDG will not be required.

You will be able to run your business without your parent’s representation when you come of age (you reach the age of 18).

 

An old age pensioner

In a nutshell:  

If you run a business as a retired person, your pension entitlement will be reduced only if you benefit from an early retirement Early retirement – an old-age entitlement for persons born between 1949 and 1968, who worked under special conditions or performed a specific type of work.

A woman can retire at the age of 60, a man – at the age of 65.

As an old-age pensioner, you can:

  • register a new business
  • continue the business started before your retirement

As an old-age pensioner running your own business, you are not subject to any limits on revenue that would result in reducing your pension entitlement.

The only exception is the so-called early retirement Early retirement – an old-age entitlement for persons born between 1949 and 1968, who worked under special conditions or performed a specific type of work. If this is your case, your retirement pension will be:

  • reduced, once your revenue exceeds 70% of the average monthly remuneration
  • suspended, once your revenue exceeds 130% of the average monthly remuneration
