Licence

When a licence is required

In a nutshell:

Certain types of business activity require a consent of the relevant authority. But before you apply for such a consent, you need to register your business.

The general rule is freedom of business activity. But, as always, there are exceptions to the rule.

Certain types of business require a consent granted by relevant public bodies.

Grant of a consent may require meeting specific conditions, such as education, age, clean criminal register, specific professional qualifications, having appropriate equipment or premises. Such conditions are usually associated with a specific type of activity.

In Poland, a consent can be given in one of the three following forms:

  • a licence
  • a permit
  • an entry in the register of regulated activities

Additionally, in the case of certain types of activities, you will need a certificate of relevant qualifications. This applies to certain professions that require specific qualifications or authorisation to practice a given profession, such as a property appraiser, a lawyer, an architect or a tax advisor. In practice, such certificate of qualifications has the same legal consequences as a permit â€“ it means that you are authorised to run a specific business and possess the required qualifications.

A licence

This is the most stringent requirement. It applies to activities that are crucial from the point of view of the state, its security, and economics.

The general categories of activities that require a licence are:

  • mining
  • production of and trade in weapons
  • production of and trade in electricity and other energy sources
  • security services
  • radio and TV broadcasting
  • running casinos
  • air transport

If your activity falls within any of the above categories, check whether you need to apply for a licence. The detailed list of activities for which a licence is required is available here.

A permit

A given activity requires a permit when it is so stated in relevant legal regulations. A permit is issued, when a potential business person is found to have abilities and technical and organizational capacities to run a particular type of business. This means that specific conditions determined by law must be met.

A permit is issued in the form of an administrative decision that may introduce special conditions to be met.

Remember:

If you meet all the conditions required for a specific business activity, you cannot be refused a permit.

Some more popular types of business activity requiring a permit are:

  • trade in alcoholic beverages
  • running a pharmacy
  • trade in products subject to excise duty
  • waste collection and processing

If your activity falls within any of the above categories, check whether you need to apply for a permit. The detailed list of activities for which a permit is required is available here.

An entry in the register of regulated activities

This is the simplest form of regulating business activity in Poland. To be entered in the relevant register, you need to:

  • meet specific conditions
  • apply for an entry in the register of regulated activities
  • declare that you meet the conditions required from a person intending to run a specific type of business (as an attachment to your application).

Remember:

You need to store any documents confirming that you meet the conditions. You will need to present them in the case of an inspection by the relevant authority.

Such registers are publicly available and can be inspected by anybody. The data disclosed, though, is limited to the necessary scope, in line with the GDPR provisions.

The major categories of activities that require an entry in the register of regulated activities are:

  • production and processing of winery products
  • storing personal and payroll files
  • trade in and processing of plant protection substances
  • detective services
  • individual medical, nursery and midwife practice
  • driver training courses
  • organising tourist events
  • telecommunications

If your activity falls within any of the above categories, check whether you need to apply for an entry in the register of regulated activities. The detailed list of activities for which an entry is required is available here.

An entity holding a register of regulated activities can refuse an entry, when:

  • a final court order was issued that prohibits you to run the business activity in question
  • you were deleted from the relevant entry following a decision prohibiting you to run the business activity within 3 years before you file your application
  • in other cases specified in detailed regulations

What to do:  

1. Check if the activity that you plan to run requires some form of consent.

2. Consult the relevant websites or documents.

3. Once you registered your business, you will need to apply for the relevant consent.
