File an application

Once you have prepared the required information and documents, you can proceed to fill in your registration application.

Access the registration application

You can access the registration form in two ways:

  • electronically
  • on paper

To access and fill in the registration form electronically, you need to use a method of confirming your identity.

You can use any of the following methods of confirming your identity:

  • profil zaufany (trusted profile)
  • e-dowód (Polish e-ID card)
  • a bank account in one of the banks that offer a confirmation of your identity
If you can use one of the above methods of confirming your identity, you can fill in the registration application online. Follow these steps:
  1. Go to website Biznes.gov.pl
  2. Click the button "Zarejestruj firmę" (Register your business)
  3. Click the button "Rozpocznij" (Start)
  4. You will have two login options:
    • Zaloguj się przez Login.gov.pl (Log in via Login.gov.pl)
    • Zaloguj się przez e-mail (Log in using our e-mail)
    5. Choose the one that you want to use.
  5. You will be asked to give your e-mail address. Any information from biznes.gov, including a confirmation of submitting your registration application, will be sent to this e-mail address. Give your valid e-email address.
  6.  You will see this page. It gives you a brief information on how to register a business. It also says that registering a business in Poland is free of charge. Click "Dalej" (Next).

Remember:  

If you are asked to make any payment, beware - you might have been redirected to a fake page.


 

You have reached the registration form online.

If you do not have any of the above methods of confirming your identity, you will need to prepare a paper version of your application. Follow these steps:
  1. Go to this page
  2. Download the form to your computer and save it.
  3. Then you can either:
  • print the form and fill it in with a pen
  • fill it in the Acrobat, using the "Fill in and sign" function. Then print it.

Remember:  

Do not sign it yet! You will need it to sign it in the presence of an officer.

Fill in the registration application

The detailed guide on how to fill in individual fields is available here.

File your registration application

If you have completed the registration application on-line, just click the Submit button.

If you have completed the registration application on paper, you can file it:

  • in person - you can deliver it to any community office in Poland, regardless of where you live.

You will need to present to an officer the original document (or its notarised copy) confirming your status of a foreigner. It can be any of the following:

  • a Polish card,
  • a permit for a temporary stay in Poland,
  • if you are married to a Polish citizen - your marriage certificate and a document confirming your spouse's identity.

The officer will scan the document and attach it to your registration application. This will accelerate processing your application.

  • by post - you can send it to any community office. If you choose this method, you must sign your registration application in the presence of a notary, who will confirm authenticity of your signature.

What next

Once you filed your registration application, the process is over.

The office will verify your application. If there are any errors in your application filed online, you will be informed by an automated message. If you filed your application in person or by post, the office will contact you and ask you to correct any errors or omissions. You will have 7 business days to do it.

If your application was correct, your business will be entered in CEIDG Centralna Informacja i Ewidencja Działalności Gospodarczej – the Central Register and Information on Economic Activity – is the Polish office run by the minister in charge of economic matters, where sole traderships and civil-law partnerships are registered.

The entry will be visible on the next business day following the date when your application was filed.

You can start your business on the same day when you filed your registration application.

CEIDG will forward your data to the relevant tax office. The office will assign you a NIP number, if you do not have it yet.

CEIDG will forward your data to the Polish Statistical Office (GUS). You will be assigned a REGON REGON is a statistical number assigned to businesses by the Polish Statistical Office (GUS). number.

CEIDG will forward your data to the Social Security Office Social Security Office – in Polish: Zakład Ubezpieczeń Społecznych, or ZUS – is the authority responsible for assessing and collecting contributions and payment of social security benefits. (ZUS).
